The 4 Core Pricing Models

Hourly Rate

$75 - $250/hour

Simple per-hour billing for work rendered. Transparent but unpredictable for clients.

Pros
  • Simple to price
  • Easy to scale
  • Predictable income
Cons
  • Caps your revenue
  • Clients resist hours
  • Hard to upsell

Monthly Retainer

$3,000 - $15,000/month

Fixed monthly fee for ongoing services. Most predictable revenue model.

Pros
  • Predictable MRR
  • Deeper relationships
  • Easier forecasting
Cons
  • Scope creep risk
  • Client churn impact
  • Hidden overheads

Project-Based

$5,000 - $50,000

Fixed price for defined project scope. Clear deliverables, clear pricing.

Pros
  • No scope creep
  • High-value work
  • Clear ROI for client
Cons
  • Unpredictable workload
  • Client acquisition cost
  • One-time projects

Performance-Based

10-30% of ad spend or revenue share

Fee based on campaign results. Aligns incentives but risky if targeting is poor.

Pros
  • Client loves alignment
  • Unlimited upside
  • Filters bad clients
Cons
  • Low upfront income
  • Risky if targeting poor
  • Complex accounting

Decision Guide: Which Model Is Right for You?

Choose Hourly If:

You're just starting out
Simple, predictable, easier to close first clients. Scale to retainer later.
Work is highly variable
Audit projects, one-off optimizations, ad hoc requests. Hourly reflects unpredictability.
You're solo or small team
Limited capacity. Hourly maximizes rate per hour of your time.

Choose Retainer If:

You want predictable revenue
MRR scales predictably. Easier to hire and forecast. Typical agency model.
You have proven expertise
Clients pay premium for ongoing optimization and results over time.
You're scaling to 10+ clients
Retainer ($5K Ã— 10 = $50K MRR) beats hourly for revenue scale.
You want deeper relationships
Ongoing work = trust, upsells, referrals. Long-term value higher.

Choose Project-Based If:

Work has clear boundaries
Landing page builds, audit reports, campaign launches. Well-defined scope.
You want high-value engagements
$20K-50K projects attract better quality clients than hourly work.
You're building a personal brand
Higher value projects build reputation better than retainers.

Choose Performance-Based If:

You're confident in results
You know you'll deliver 3-5x ROI. Risk is acceptable for unlimited upside.
Clients have deep pockets
High-spend accounts ($100K+ ad spend/month). Your upside is substantial.
You want to filter for ideal clients
Only confident, well-funded clients accept performance deals.

Hybrid Models (Best of Both Worlds)

Model 1: Retainer + Performance Bonus $5K retainer + 10% of incremental revenue above baseline

Gives predictable base + upside for amazing results. Clients love shared risk.

Model 2: Project + Retainer $15K landing page build + $2K/month optimization retainer

Front-loads revenue from build, then locks in recurring MRR for optimization.

Model 3: Tiered Retainer $3K base + $500 per client if managing 3+ accounts simultaneously

Rewards scale. Clients on same team pay less. Best for multi-client agencies.

Model 4: Value-Based Fixed fee based on expected ROI, not hours or ad spend

If client is spending $100K/month and sees $400K revenue, you charge % of uplift.

Common Questions

Should I start hourly or retainer? +
Start hourly (easier to close). After 3-5 successful projects, transition to retainer with existing clients. Move new prospects directly to retainer. Your confidence and track record justify it.
How do I avoid scope creep on retainers? +
Define exactly what's included: "10 hours/month of optimization, 2 strategy calls, monthly reporting." Anything over = overage at $150/hr or additional project fee. Document in contract.
What if I miss performance targets? +
Performance-based works best when: (1) You control the variables, (2) Client has quality traffic already, (3) It's measured over 90+ days. Agree on baselines upfront.
How much should I charge? +
Charge 3-5x what you think the value is. If a $5K landing page generates $50K in revenue for the client, charge $5K, not $2K. You're undercutting your value.
Can I change pricing for existing clients? +
Yes, at contract renewal (typically 12 months). Give 30-60 days notice. Most clients accept 5-10% annual increases if you've delivered results. Position it as market rate adjustment.
